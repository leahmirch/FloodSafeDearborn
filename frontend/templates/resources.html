Resources

Welcome to the resources section of FloodSafeDearborn. This section provides critical information and links to help you prepare for, respond to, and recover from flooding events. Below, you’ll find contact details for relevant authorities, tips to protect your home, and resources for staying safe during emergencies.

Emergency Contacts

  • Wayne County Drain Commissioner’s Office:

    Contact the Wayne County Drain Commissioner’s Office for drainage issues and flood-related concerns in Wayne County, MI.

    Visit Wayne County Drain Commissioner’s Website
  • Michigan Department of Natural Resources (DNR):

    Get resources and information about flood zones and environmental management.

    Visit Michigan DNR Website
  • MDOT (Michigan Department of Transportation):

    Access real-time road conditions and emergency route information.

    Visit MDOT Website

Tips to Protect Yourself and Your Home

  • Prepare an Emergency Kit: Include essentials like water, non-perishable food, flashlight, batteries, first aid supplies, and medications.
  • Protect Your Property: Elevate electrical appliances, seal walls with waterproofing materials, and ensure your sump pump is operational.
  • Stay Informed: Sign up for local weather alerts and notifications. FloodSafeDearborn offers real-time notifications for registered users.
  • Evacuation Plans: Know your evacuation routes and communicate with family members about where to go in case of a flood.

Local Assistance

If you need immediate assistance during a flood, please contact local emergency services by dialing 911. For non-emergency issues related to flooding, you can reach out to the Wayne County Drain Commissioner’s Office for guidance and support.
