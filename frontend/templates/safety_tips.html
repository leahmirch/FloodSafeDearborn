Flood Safety Tips

Flooding can be dangerous and cause significant damage to property and communities. Being prepared and knowing how to respond can save lives and reduce losses. Below, youâ€™ll find practical tips to help protect yourself, your home, and your community from the effects of flooding, along with links to valuable resources for further guidance.

Protecting Yourself

  • Stay Informed: Monitor local weather reports and flood alerts through reliable sources like Weather.gov.
  • Evacuate Early: If authorities advise evacuation, do so promptly and follow established evacuation routes.
  • Avoid Floodwaters: Never walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters. Just six inches of moving water can knock you off your feet, and one foot of water can sweep away vehicles.
  • Emergency Kit: Prepare an emergency kit that includes water, non-perishable food, a flashlight, batteries, a first-aid kit, medications, and important documents.

Protecting Your Home

  • Waterproof Your Basement: Seal basement walls with waterproofing compounds to prevent water seepage.
  • Install Sump Pumps: Use a sump pump to help drain water from your basement. Consider a battery-powered backup in case of power outages.
  • Elevate Utilities: Raise electrical appliances, furnaces, and other utilities above expected flood levels to prevent damage.
  • Clear Gutters and Drains: Regularly clean gutters and storm drains to ensure proper water flow and prevent clogs.
  • Sandbags: Use sandbags to divert water away from your property in the event of a flood warning.

Protecting Your Community

  • Report Flooding: Notify local authorities and platforms like FloodSafeDearborn about flooding in your area to help others stay informed.
  • Support Green Infrastructure: Advocate for rain gardens, permeable pavements, and other measures that improve stormwater management in your community.
  • Volunteer: Join local initiatives to assist with flood preparedness and recovery efforts.

Additional Resources

  • FEMA: Flood Maps and Safety Tips
  • EPA: Green Infrastructure

Emergency Contacts

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger during a flood, call 911. For non-emergency flood-related assistance, contact the Wayne County Drain Commissioner or visit the Resources page for more information.
